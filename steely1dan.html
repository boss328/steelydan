    Steely Dan is a yacht rock (a genre of soft rock which peaked in popularity in the mid-70s and 80s) band whose members were Walter Becker and Donald Fagen, the geniuses behind all their songs. They have earned a Grammy Award for Album of the Year, Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album twice, and were entered into the Grammy Hall of Fame.

    Steely Dan had album sales of 40 million and some of their songs like Do it Again, if you just hear a little clip of it, are stuck in your head for the rest of the day. The amount of Grammys that they have received is astounding, especially for the time. They spark joy in the listener by just the sound of their music because of the uniqueness of the sound for the time and the change they inspired.

    SD’s (Steely Dan) album "Aja" was one of or arguably the best album they created. They spent 1 year creating it. During that time they recorded it and mixed. It was recorded alongside 40 musicians, who were well known and respected. The album consists of 7 songs and the duration is only 40 minutes. They have received many awards and honors for "Aja". They earned a Grammy Award for Best Engineered Recording - Non-Classical and received Grammy nominations for Album of the Year and Best Pop Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals. One more interesting fact is that the album was entered into the Library of Congress for being "culturally, historically, or artistically significant."

    Gaucho, their next album, was recorded in a 2-year span they had over 42 musicians play. SD changed their sound to a more minimal groove sound. This was so because of the harmonically complex chord changes that were recognizable in older albums and songs. Post-Gaucho SD went on a 20y recording hiatus.

    Lastly, SD inspires introspective anti-capitalism. This isn’t music you would sing at a march. It’s not going to make you want to get out and protest, but it will certainly make you think twice about the corruption in society. It is only if you carefully analyze the lyrics that you can find these hidden meanings.
